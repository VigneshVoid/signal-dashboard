🔥 Must-ReadMar 15, 2026 · arXiv:2603.14482
V-JEPA 2.1 — Dense Features, +20 Points Robot Grasping, New SOTA on Anticipation
V-JEPA 2.1 drops four key upgrades over V-JEPA 2: (1) Dense predictive loss — both visible and masked tokens contribute to training signal, forcing explicit spatial/temporal grounding. (2) Deep self-supervision applied hierarchically across multiple encoder layers. (3) Multi-modal tokenizers for unified image+video training. (4) Effective scaling in model capacity and data. Results: 7.71 mAP on Ego4D short-term object anticipation · 40.8 Recall@5 on EPIC-KITCHENS (up from 39.7 in V-JEPA 2) · +20pt real-robot grasping success rate · 77.7 on Something-Something-V2 · 5.687 ATE on TartanDrive robot navigation.
Direct Week 1–24 curriculum relevance
This is the live successor to your Week 24 north-star paper. Read V-JEPA 2 to understand the foundation, V-JEPA 2.1 to see where it evolved. The dense predictive loss is the most significant architectural insight — it's what changes the masking objective from self-supervised "fill in the blank" to genuine spatial grounding.
ResearchMar 23, 2026 · LeCun et al.
LeWorldModel (LeWM) — First JEPA to Train End-to-End from Pixels Without Representation Collapse
LeCun, Balestriero et al. (Mila, NYU, Samsung SAIL, Brown) introduce LeWM: the first JEPA that trains stably end-to-end from raw pixels using only two loss terms — a next-embedding prediction loss and SIGReg, a regularizer enforcing Gaussian-distributed latents via the Cramér-Wold theorem. Previous JEPAs needed stop-gradients, EMA, and frozen encoders to avoid collapse. LeWM eliminates all that — just one tunable hyperparameter versus six. ~15M parameters, trainable on a single GPU in hours, plans 48× faster than foundation-model-based world models.
Key insight for your research
Representation collapse is JEPA's core fragility — why early versions needed EMA tricks. LeWM's SIGReg solution is mathematically elegant and will be essential to understand when you hit the architecture deep-dive weeks. The "why does JEPA need EMA?" question has a clean answer now.
ResearchJan 2026 · ICLR 2026
VL-JEPA — Vision-Language JEPA, 50% Fewer Parameters, Accepted at ICLR 2026
Meta's VL-JEPA applies JEPA to vision-language: instead of generating tokens autoregressively, it predicts continuous embeddings of target texts. Results: stronger than standard VLMs with 50% fewer trainable parameters. Selective decoding reduces operations by 2.85× with no accuracy loss. Architecture: V-JEPA 2 as vision encoder, Llama 3 layers as predictor, EmbeddingGemma-300M as target encoder. Supports open-vocabulary classification, retrieval, and VQA from one model without architecture changes.

Research ContextMar 2026
AMI vs. World Labs — Two Bets on the Same Thesis, Different Entry Points
Both labs share the same critique: generative LLMs predict tokens, not reality. LLMs excel at text, code, math — discrete, low-dimensional, predictable. But physical sensor data (video, robot sensors, 3D) is fundamentally unpredictable at the pixel level — generative approaches break down. World models learn abstract representations of environments, ignore unpredictable surface noise, predict in latent space. Action-conditioned variants let agents simulate consequences of actions and plan sequences — the missing link for robotics and autonomous systems.

AMI (LeCun): JEPA-native, abstract latent prediction, targets healthcare/robotics/industrial automation, open research. World Labs (Li): spatial/3D intelligence, 3D generation and reasoning, creative/design tools first, robotics as the prize. Different entry points — same endgame.
