The Compute Arms Race Has a Price Tag — And a Political Agenda
Two stories dominated the week and they are more connected than they look. First: Anthropic locked in 3.5 gigawatts of compute from Google and Broadcom — an expansion of last October's deal — as run-rate revenue surged to $30B and enterprise customers spending over $1M annually doubled to 1,000 in under two months. The model is working and the company is scaling infrastructure to match it.

Second: OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google quietly began sharing threat intelligence through the Frontier Model Forum to identify and block adversarial distillation — Chinese competitors extracting model capabilities through carefully crafted queries in violation of terms of service. The announcement (Bloomberg, Apr 6) signals the labs have concluded that capability leakage is a real risk worth coordinating on, even between direct competitors.

Underneath both stories: the more capable and commercially valuable these models become, the more the compute race, the IP protection game, and geopolitical maneuvering all merge into a single strategic problem. The week's other highlights — TurboQuant at ICLR 2026 halving inference costs, LillyPod bringing 1,016 Blackwell Ultras to drug discovery, Muse Spark landing #4 on the Artificial Analysis leaderboard — are table-stakes for a field moving at this pace.
Signal for your research
TurboQuant (Google, ICLR 2026) addresses the KV cache bottleneck that limits long-context inference — directly relevant when you start running 1M-context JEPA research sessions. Bookmark the paper now; it will matter by Week 12 of your curriculum when you're working with full video sequences.

AnnouncementApr 6, 2026
OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Form Anti-Distillation Coalition — Sharing Intel on Chinese Model Copying via Frontier Model Forum
The three US AI labs have begun coordinating through the Frontier Model Forum to detect and block adversarial distillation — attempts by third parties (primarily Chinese competitors) to extract frontier capabilities by querying public-facing models with crafted prompts. Labs are sharing detection heuristics and flagged query patterns. This marks the first time direct commercial competitors have collaborated on threat intelligence. Terms of service violations are the legal lever; actual enforcement is still nascent.
Broader significance
If model distillation can be reliably detected and blocked, it tilts the capability advantage back toward compute-rich incumbents. If not, open-weight variants and distilled models will keep compressing the moat. This coalition is a bet on detection being tractable.

TurboQuant — Google's KV Cache Algorithm Cuts Memory 6× at ICLR 2026, Biggest Inference Win of the Cycle
Google Research unveiled TurboQuant at ICLR 2026, an algorithm that dramatically reduces the memory overhead of the KV cache — one of the hardest bottlenecks in long-context inference. The KV cache stores key/value attention states for each token in a context window; at 1M+ token contexts it balloons to dominate GPU memory budgets. TurboQuant compresses KV representations without meaningful accuracy loss, delivering a reported 6× memory reduction. Practical impact: the same hardware can now serve 6× more concurrent long-context sessions, or the same session count at 6× the context length.
For your research sessions
You're currently running Sonnet 4.6 with 1M context natively. TurboQuant is the infrastructure reason that context window became economically viable to offer. When you start pasting full JEPA papers mid-session, this is what makes it not collapse.
